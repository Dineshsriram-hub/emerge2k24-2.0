ABOUT EMERGE2K24-2.0

SRM Valliammai Engineering College, Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE), proudly presents the 14th National Level Technical Symposium, EMERGE 2K24-2.0 . This prestigious event is set to offer an exhilarating platform for students to showcase their technical acumen and creativity across five distinct events:

  1. Paper Presentation
  2. Project Expo
  3. Circuitrix
  4. Debugging
  5. Treasure Hunt

For Paper Presentation and Project Expo, a maximum of four members per team is allowed, while for the other events, teams can comprise up to three participants.The registration fee is ₹150 per participant. Participants will enjoy complimentary food, and every participant will receive E-Certificate as recognition for their involvement.

DEBUGGING

Debugging is an innovative and thrilling challenge that blends your technical skills, creativity, and teamwork to test your problem-solving abilities. Together, participants will solve and repair malfunctioning circuits, presenting humorous and surprising turns. You'll be forced to think creatively and interact with actual circuits in each round, whether it's solving questions, overcoming challenges, or clocking out. This event is sure to be an exciting experience for everyone involved, filled with interactive ingredients, hilarious moments, and plenty of surprises along the way!

GUIDELINES:



Debugging event will be full of surprises in each round still to make it more fun we expect everyone to follow the following guidelines:

  1. The maximum number of participants should be 3 .
  2. Taking help from others or mobile phones will not be allowed.
  3. In case of any malpractice by any member of the team then the whole team will be disqualified.
  4. Final selection will be based on the performance of every team

ROUNDS:



Total of three (3) rounds will be there and the final winner will be chosen after the third round (Each round will be an elimination round).


Round 1: Meme circuit


In the first round a humorous, intentionally faulty circuit based on a popular meme or joke within the electronics community will be given. Participants must debug the circuit while enjoying the humor behind its design.


Round 2:Mystery box challenge


n second round the participants will be provided with a "mystery box" containing a pre-assembled circuit with hidden faults. The box could contain incorrect components or connections that the participants need to identify .


Round 3: Reverse engineering


In the final round the participants will be given a fully functioning circuit and will be asked to intentionally create faults based on certain criteria. Other teams will then swap circuits and attempt to debug the newly "broken" circuits. (The team will completes both the tasks first will be the winner and the next will be the runner up.)
